Course Overviewüëæ

The "Design and Analysis of Algorithms" course provides an in-depth understanding of the principles behind algorithm design and data structure utilization. It explores their applications and optimization techniques.
Beginning with foundational topics like space and time complexity,Efficiency analysis, recursion, and backtracking, the course progresses into specialized tools such as hashing and Union-Find. It also examines advanced structures including AVL trees, Segment Trees, and Red-Black Trees. Sorting and searching strategies, from Quick Sort to KMP, enhance our grasp of efficient data handling.
Additionally, graph algorithms like Dijkstra's and Prim's demonstrate real-world problem-solving, while topics like NP-completeness offer insights into the boundaries of computational power. By the end, students build strong algorithmic thinking and problem-solving skills applicable across diverse scenarios.

Foundational Mathematics:

We began our journey by studying the mathematical principles that underpin algorithms. Topics included calculating time and space complexities and solving recurrence relations, with practical examples like the Tower of Hanoi and the N-Queens problem.

Growth and Efficiency:

We explored growth orders to better understand algorithm efficiency and evaluate solutions in greater depth.

Structured Data Handling:

We delved into structured data, examining binary trees, which evolved into binary search trees. From there, we studied advanced trees like 2-3 Trees, AVL Trees for balancing, and Red-Black Trees for enhanced performance. We also learned about Tries, Skip Lists, and Binary Indexed Trees (Fenwick Trees) for specific applications, alongside graph traversal methods like DFS and BFS.
